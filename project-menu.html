Menu Case Study

UX Research | Rebranding

Sketch, Adobe Illustrator

tl;dr

Why do I keep ordering the same dishes?
I don't know what to order from the menu.

Restaurant menu is one of the first user-facing artifacts that we encounter as customers. There are inherently many common problems pertaining to how menus display the dishes that lead to questions that we sometimes don't want to bother and ask the waiter/waitress. In order to make this experience more pleasant and intuitive for customers, I identified such problems and redesigned the menu to reflect my findings.

Research

For this typical case, I firmly believe that observation and direct communication with the customers will yield significant insights into my redesign decision. Without a doubt, a majority of customers, including me, shared very common menu firsthand experience:

  • Overwhelming lists of dishes (there are actually 10 pages in total!)
  • Not clear description of what ingredients are in the dishes
  • Repetitive and unnecessary names for some dishes
  • Not appealing overall look and feel
